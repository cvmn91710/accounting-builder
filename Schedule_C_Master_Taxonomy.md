# Schedule C — Master Disbursement Taxonomy

**Purpose:** Standardized chart of accounts for Schedule C (Disbursements) across probate, trust administration, and conservatorship accountings. Built from analysis of five GOLGLLP accountings (14529 Evans, 22465 Ward, 8788 Green, 2223 Bartolotta x2).

**Convention:** Parent category → Subcategory → (Payee/line item). Use the parent for subtotals on the schedule. Group transactions by *expense type*, not by payee, except where a single payee dominates a category (e.g., a single facility under Residential Care).

**Matter-type applicability:** Each parent category is tagged with which matter type(s) it applies to:
- **P** = Probate
- **T** = Trust Administration
- **C** = Conservatorship / Guardianship

---

## 1. Real Property Expenses — Ongoing  *(P, T, C)*

Expenses to maintain real property held by the estate/trust during administration. Distinct from sale-related closing costs.

- Mortgage Payments (P&I)
- Property Tax (regular installments)
- Supplemental Property Tax
- Homeowners Insurance (annual premium)
- HOA Dues & Special Assessments
- Utilities (gas, electric, water, sewer, trash)
- Telephone / Internet
- Security / Alarm Monitoring (e.g., ADT)
- Landscaping / Yard Maintenance
- House Cleaning
- Repairs & Maintenance
- Property Management Fees
- Pool / Pest Control
- Trash-Out / Estate Cleanout
- Rekey / Securing the Property
- Rent (when property is leased *to* the estate, e.g., conservatee's apartment)

- Notary / Mobile Notary Fee

## 21. Refunds, Reversals & Adjustments  *(P, T, C)*

- Refunds Received (negative disbursement)
- Reversed Charges
- Bank Error Corrections
- Overpayment Recovery

*(Best practice: do **not** bury negative entries under the original payee. Break them out so reviewers can see net activity.)*

---

## 22. Miscellaneous / Other  *(P, T, C)*

Use sparingly. Anything that lands here for more than one or two transactions probably deserves its own parent category. Common one-offs:

- Pet Final Expenses
- Lost / Damaged Property Replacement
- Settlement of Disputed Claims (non-creditor)
- One-Time Vendor Payments

## Design notes

1. **Group by type, not by payee.** Of the five sample accountings, only File 2 (Ward) grouped one-subcategory-per-payee. That style produces 50+ subtotals on a single schedule and makes year-over-year comparison nearly impossible. Group by expense **type**; let the description column carry the payee.

2. **Break out tax payments.** Tax payments to the IRS, FTB, and county tax collector buried inside "Other Expenses" obscure a meaningful number. Pull them into Section 12.

3. **Don't put a sale inside Other.** Real property sale closing costs should always be their own parent (Section 2), even when the sale is the only real estate transaction in the period.

4. **Negatives need a home.** Refunds and reversals get their own subcategory (Section 21), not buried as negative amounts under the original payee.

5. **Fiduciary comp + attorney fees on the schedule.** California requires these to be requested and approved by the court in probate; the disbursement (when paid) belongs on Schedule C with a clear reference to the order approving payment.

6. **Per-matter customization.** Not every matter uses every parent. Suppress empty parents from the final schedule rather than showing zero subtotals — but keep them in the master template so nothing is forgotten during prep.
